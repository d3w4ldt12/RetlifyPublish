How SightLine Works

SightLine works by tracking the position of your pupils and then determining what you are looking at based on where they are facing. In order to use this tool, you must complete the calibration feature. This works by having you look at a dot on the screen and then clicking once. Once the current is calibrated, you can now move the mouse based on your face.

Key Features

  • Advanced Eye-Tracking Technology

    - Utilizes cutting-edge algorithms to detect and follow eye movement with remarkable precision, converting natural gaze patterns into actionable commands.

    - Tracks eye movement seamlessly across the screen, ensuring every interaction is accurately captured, allowing users to navigate intuitively and effectively.

  • Functionality

    Left Click with Two Blinks: When the system detects two quick blinks in succession, it simulates a left mouse click. This could be used for actions such as selecting items, opening files, or activating buttons.

    Right Click with Three Blinks: Three quick blinks would trigger a right mouse click, allowing users to access context menus or secondary actions that typically require a right-click.

  • Calibration

    Allows users to calibrate the blink detection sensitivity to accommodate individual differences in blink rate and duration.

  • User-Friendly Interface

    Simple, intuitive design that allows easy navigation for users of all abilities.

  • Data Privacy and Security

    Clear policies on data handling, ensuring user privacy and protection of personal information.

    Adaptive tracking algorithms: Optimize algorithms to adapt to different types of glasses, including reflective and anti-reflective lenses, which often interfere with eye-tracking accuracy.
